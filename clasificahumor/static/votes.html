1. Genre

First, we want to know if the text is supposed to be a joke or not. So the first question is:

Is the intention of this text to be humorous?

Yes or No

Even if you don’t personally find the joke funny, you can normally tell if the person who wrote it intended it as a joke. We call this the ‘genre’ of the text, and it's relatively objective. If you say ‘yes, this text was probably intended to be humorous’, after that we’ll ask you:

How humorous do you find the text?

Scale from 1 (not funny at all) to 5 (very funny), or “I don’t get it”

Here you can give your personal response: this is subjective and is completely up to you.

1. Genre

Let’s look at an example:

Anna reads a text and decides it’s supposed to be a joke and actually she finds it really funny.

- For the question ‘Is the intention of this text to be humorous?’ she answers yes.

- For the question ‘How humorous do you find the text?’, she answers 5 (very funny).

John looks at the same text, think ‘oof that’s a bad joke.’ He can tell it’s supposed to be a joke, but he doesn’t find it funny at all.

- For the question ‘Is the intention of this text to be humorous?’ he answers yes.

- For the question ‘How humorous do you find the text?’, he answers 1 (not funny at all).

This shows that although John didn’t like the joke, he still can recognise that it was intended to be humorous.

Sometimes you can tell it’s supposed to be a joke, but you just don’t get it. Don’t worry, you can select ‘yes, it’s intended to be a joke’ and then selected ‘I don’t get it’.

So, what’s the difference between a rating of 1, and a rating of ‘I don’t get it’? Rating the text as ‘1’ means you just don’t think it’s funny, where ‘I don’t get it’ means that you think it should be funny, but you’re not sure why. We’re just interested in your honest opinion here.

If that's clear, please move on to the next page of instructions.

2. Generally Offensive

The next question you’ll be asked is:

Is this text generally offensive?

Yes or No

By this we mean, does it target a person or group of people just because they belong to that group? You can also think of it as ‘would a lot of people be offended by this joke?’. If you think the answer is yes, you'll be asked:

How generally offensive do you find the text?

Scale from 1 (mildly offensive) to 5 (extremely offensive)

To what extent do you think people would be generally offended by the content of the tweet? Please select a rating from 1-5.

Click next to see an example.

2. Generally Offensive

For example:

John sees a joke that he thinks a lot of people might take offense to, but he also thinks that most people would see it as a little bit of humor that wouldn’t hurt much.

- For the question ‘Is this text generally offensive?’ he answers yes.

- For the question ‘How generally offensive do you find the text?’, he answers 1.

Anna also thinks the joke makes fun of a group of people, but thinks it’s kind of mean-spirited, so she selects ‘4’.

- For the question ‘Is this text generally offensive?’ she answers yes.

- For the question ‘How generally offensive do you find the text?’, she answers 4.

For some jokes, you might think ‘I find this hilarious, but some people might be offended by it.’ Don’t worry! Some jokes which are offensive might also make you laugh. We’re just interested in your opinion. If you think it’s just a joke, and not really offensive, select no. Maybe it talks about a group of people, but not in a negative way. Maybe it’s just innocent humor.

What if you’re not sure if it’s offensive? Best to just say no.

3. Personally Offensive

The last question will be:

Do you find this text personally offensive?

Yes or No

We want to know if this joke offends you, personally. If you belong to the group targeted by the joke, but you don’t really care, select no. If you belong to the group targeted and you do find it hurtful, select yes. Or maybe you don’t belong to the group targeted, but you feel hurt on behalf of someone else, you can select ‘yes’.

Again, there are no wrong answers.

If you select yes, you'll then answer:

How personally offensive do you find this text?

Scale from 1 (mildly offensive) to 5 (extremely offensive)

To what extent are you affected or personally offended by the content of the tweet? Please select a rating from 1-5.
